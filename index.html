Understanding Your IP Address

Every device online gets an IP address — a number that tells the internet where to send data. It works like a postal code: not exact enough for your front door, but enough to get data to the right area.

IPv4 vs IPv6

IPv4 looks like 192.168.1.1 and only has about 4.3 billion possible addresses — most are already used up. IPv6 is a longer format that gives virtually unlimited addresses for the growing number of connected devices.

Public vs Private IP

A private IP only works inside your home network. A public IP is the one the internet sees, usually shared by all your devices through your router.

Why IP Location Isn't Exact

IP location is estimated from provider records, not GPS. It's usually right at the country level, often right at the city level, but never precise enough for a street address.

Static vs Dynamic IP

A static IP stays fixed, common for servers. A dynamic IP changes now and then — normal for most home internet.

Protecting Your Privacy

Your IP is visible to every site you visit. Here's how people hide it.

VPN

Routes and encrypts your traffic through another server, so sites see that server's IP instead of yours.

Proxy

Also hides your IP, but usually without encryption — faster, less secure than a VPN.

Tor vs VPN

Tor bounces traffic through several relays for stronger anonymity, but it's slower. A VPN is quicker for daily use.

Quick Glossary

Networking Terms

ISP

The company that connects you to the internet and hands out your public IP.

Tier 1 vs Tier 2

Tier 1 providers own the backbone. Tier 2 providers usually pay to connect through them.

Example

A local cable company is typically Tier 2, buying bandwidth from a bigger backbone operator.

ASN

A number that identifies a network operator — like an ISP — used for internet routing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is my IP address?

The number your internet provider gives your device so it can send and receive data online. It's shown automatically on this page.

Is my IP address my exact location?

No. It points to your rough city or region, not your street address, and accuracy varies by provider.

Can someone track me using my IP?

By itself, an IP only shows your rough region and provider, not your identity. A VPN can hide it for extra privacy.

Why does my IP address change?

Most home connections use dynamic IPs that your provider reassigns from time to time, including after a router restart.

IPv4 vs IPv6 — what's the difference?

IPv4 addresses are shorter and nearly used up. IPv6 is a longer format built to support far more devices.
